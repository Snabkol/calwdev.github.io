Game Project 1 - Call of Dinner

Developed by: Stewdio



Pitch, 09-11-2022:
Today we were introduced to the team for the first game project. Me and Emma held our game pitches and let the team vote on their favourite candidate in a Google Form we set up for the pitches. The game which was picked was Emma's "Call of Dinner". I look forward to working with these people, and the game idea is great so I have a good feeling about this.

Day 1, 14-11-2022:
Pre-production of "Call of Dinner" began today. An art-, programmer-, and design-lead were appointed. These will be responsible for communication between the teams. We also discussed workflow and scope. Artists and Lead-designer had a discussion regarding artstyle and player perspective and scale.

Art by: Daniel Edskog

Day 2-5, 15th to 18th-11-2022:
I was abscent from the work as I was attending a funeral. Still had contact via Discord answering queries and supplying feedback. Meanwhile, the team set up SourceControl, started producing basic models for props and enemies as well as improving the planning for the next coming weeks.

Day 6, 21-11-2022:
Started with a level block-out today. We assigned Loke as our Environment Artist to collaborate in the level. Linus helped me set up GitFork and I managed to get into the project proper. At 2PM Emma held sprint review #1. Here on out I am going to hold the sprint reviews but seeing my absence last week we decided against it this time.

Day 7, 22-11-2022:
Finished the level block-out today, started the communication between me and our Environment Artist. I was also tasked with creating a creative template for the future Call of Dinner Sprint Reviews, which I completed. It now looks really clean while still in the style of the game. Also created a TEMP_Player to test the scale of the level and assets.

Day 8, 23-11-2022:
Emma was home sick today. I, also feeling under the weather managed to throw together a screenflow prototype with working buttons. More and more props are being finished and I can more clearly see how the game will pan out. It is a pretty cool experience. Being the sole designer today I felt more responsbility and having to take more decisions which, to be honest; I appreciate.

Day 9, 24-11-2022:
The sickness has spread. I was bed-ridden most of today with a terrible man-cold. Looked over and updated the GDD thorougly with the new direction we're taking the game, no more multiplayer and lowered the scope until further notice. It's quite interesting to see how much the direction of a game can change within just the first week of production. Hopefully we can keep the current planned content but I do honestly believe we need to pickup the pace quite substantially in order to have a polished game by Christmas.

Day 10, 25-11-2022:
Still home recovering. Have been preparing the sprint review for next week. I get so restless being home during a project like this, I want to be there and hands-on seeing what is going on and take part of the experience without the slow Discord communication.

Day 11, 28-11-2022:
Back to Playgroundsquad. Held a meeting where we planned out the workflow for the week among the entire team. I fixed up the sprint review for the previous week and presented our current planning. While my approach to keep it short and concise was not upheld by the other groups I still appreciate this way of presenting more than having elaborate info-dumps of melatonin production. The presentation went fine, but I was reminded of the Milestone due at the end of the week. Stress has re-entered my body!

Day 12, 29-11-2022:
Decided to play artist today. I taught myself how to do VFX and managed to create a styalized smoke pillar for the cooking of food. After stressing the programmers a little we managed to get the player controller to work. Other than that today has been mostly managing and checking on the other disciplines. Tomorrow's goal is to have the prototype Milestone Build ready for playtesting.

Day 13,30-11-2022:
Worked on more VFX today. Making muzzle fire is much more tedious than a pillar of smoke so I had to try to learn from tutorials which were not compatible with the engine version I am using making me waste a couple of hours of work. It's very frustrating to have little to no progress during a day like this. But I can't let that stress affect me, tomorrow we implement all the systems, assets, win & lose condition. I would be lying if I didn't say that I was worried.

Day 14, 01-12-2022:
Today we spent all day implementing the systems made by the programmers, adding assets made by the artists and swearing loudly and often at Source Control, with merge errors. Check out errors, lost progress and countless exclamations of "But it works on my screen!". At the time of writing today's entry the time is 21:15 and we will be working for an hour or two more. Hopefully we have enough of a prototype for tomorrow's milestone presentation but I am not entirely sure seeing how currently we have a good looking scene with no mechanics other than running and spawning in enemies with no inherent purpose. I feel like this might be caused by us being handicapped by the assignment, but also that I have not been managing the work as much as I maybe should've? I am quite frankly exhausted right now and I realize why game developers burn out on a frequent basis. Not that I am there yet, whenever we have a breakthrough I get back most of my motivation until Unreal has its 7th consecutive crash.

Day 15, 02-12-2022:
| Milestone day |
Had our first (and only) milestone for this project. Got to know ~1h before we were-a go that there was supposed to be some sort of visual presentation outside of the first build. This lack of communication, combined with me being the sole Designer on-site AND the long night yesterday had be very stressed and anxious. Thankfully I have great friends and Supervisor Martin had a calming talk with me which also helped get me down to earth. The presentation went very well with jokes and banter scattered in; and despite building the game (and having Albin fix the fatal errors 5 minutes beforehand) we managed to show off a stable build of the game! My anxiety is much better now afterwards, we have internally summarised what we have left to do on the game and I think we will reach a pretty good state by the end of all of this.

Day 16, 05-12-2022:
Back at it for another Monday. I have been working with VFX, got muzzle flash to work and got a basic splatter for when my enemies are shot. We set a deadline for feature-programming to be done on Friday. Might be able to keep that up. Started setting up the player HUD which will probably be my work for the week.

Day 17, 06-12-2022:
Spent the day implementing the UI from Kade, making the healthbar functional and getting the AmmoCount on the screen. I also had a discussion with another group about some ineherent design problems their game has and how they should not be adding new features to solve them, but rather look and change what they already have. Programmers have started implementing final (but non-optimized) versions of systems onto the Main Level in UE5. We will soon have a very playable prototype!

Day 18, 07-12-2022:
Healthbar and AmmoCount are fully implemented, working as intended and looking real good! Got to see the tomato animations in action as well, they are super cute and I can really feel the project coming together! Next up while waiting for more UI assets I am going to be working on implementing the VFX I made last week so that they work in tandem with all the other functionality.

Day 19 & 20, 08/09-12-2022:
Was home with a migraine, managed to find some suitable music for the main screen and in-game. Not very productive otherwise.

Day 21, 12-12-2022:
Sprint 4 today! We showed off the interactable system in the latest build. This week we will be working really hard to implement all of the systems and make sure that they work in tandem with each other and that they are fun to interact with. I need to rework the muzzleflash VFX to make sure that it really feels nice. For this week; Player feedback is all the rage, I have a feeling it might not be as good as envisioned; but I will definitely be proud over this!

Day 22, 13-12-2022:
Good progress today, the gameplay loop is being implemented at lightning speed and I have been working hard on tomato splatter VFX while Loke took over the muzzle flash. Tomorrow at 5 is deadline for Playtest Build 1, this is going to be hectic but I belive we can do it if we work a little bit overtime. All the assets are finally coming together and despite be going crazy over variables not being Blueprint accessible the work is inspiring! This is turning out great!

Day 23, 14-12-2022:
Started implementing sounds, animations & systems onto the main assets today. Got PlayerShooting, PlayerMelee & PlayerPickup to work with good looping and blending, in my opinion. The game immediately feels more satisfying to play with even this little feedback and I really understand why there has been emphasis on said feedback.

Day 24, 15-12-2022:
Continued implementing sounds and animations got PlayerReload to work in tandem with a reload sound with scuffed designer solutions in Blueprints!

Day 25, 16-12-2022:
We had to cancel our planned playtest last minute as a gamebreaking bug emerged which we didn't know how to fix. Safe to say that after fiddling with it for about 2h we took an "early" day off for mental recovery.

Day 27, 19-12-2022:
Kept implementing sounds and fiddling with numbers and prepared to hold the final sprint review! After fixing the bug from last Friday we got a build going with almost all feedback and planned features added. The game is playable and the build we showed off during the sprint has been sent out to friends and family as a playtest build so we can get some last minute feedback on Call of Dinner!
The PT build can be found here: Build
Playtest Feedback form: Form

Day 28, 20-12-2022:
Final touches on UI animations and variables. Today only me and programmer Albin were present on the technical side of the game. So we had a lot of work to do fiddling with all the smaller things, making sure nothing had broken down during our persistent development of the game. All sounds should now be implemented and work "properly", we have done all the sounds as 2D and it creates some issued but that it how it is right now. It's looking bright for the GOLD milestone of the game!

Day 29, 21-12-2022:
Final touches on EVERYTHING today. Finished the UI, finished the variables. Made a Gold Presentation and added a ton of props in the main level. The game is built and ready for "shipping" tomorrow. I don't know if I am ready for presenting and letting the game go however. Had some last minute rendering issued with the tomatoes' weapons, but we did a butchered fix and now it works!

Day 30, 22-12-2022
| GOLD MILESTONE |
Held the game Gold presentation. It went well and everyone was happy to be done with the game, and to see the other games which were on display. Such a great ending to a great project. Thanks to the Stewdio team for being awesome and communicative, and thanks to Playgroundsquad to enable us to create art.

Game Project 2 - Alien Intern VR

Developed by: Trolley Problem Productions


Day 1, 20-03-2023:
Pre-production started yesterday. I pitched the game twice, once for the main team and once for the sound crew. We all set up Milanote, Pinterest, started writing on the GDD and so much more. This week of pre-prod is going to be interesting but rewarding I think. The team seems excited to get started.

Day 2, 21-03-2023:
Started up an Asset List today. The COO of Tension held a Kick-off for the group where we got to discuss goals, plans & expectations. Felt like a neccessary evil but incredibly boring and personally unproductive. Afterwards Designers had a little meeting which started off as a clarification of roles and ended up being a solidification of roles. I am now officially Project Manager, Lead Designer & Lines of Communications officer (or whatever that title would be called) It is going to be an interesting project this. I think we'll get some great work in these 9 weeks.

Day 3, 22-03-2023:
Started the day with a meeting with the sound engineers, getting them up to speed; setting roles and expectations! The rest of the day went into answering questions regarding characters and level layout. Streamlining the Milanote & newly set up Miro moodboard. For tomorrow we are just going sit down and spew out a million modules.

Day 4, 23-03-2023:
Today has been spent coming up with new modules; figuring out how menus might (or might not work), Downloaded the engine, set up Git and did my first pull/push of the project. Good vibes.

Day 5, 24-03-2023:
Today was rather productive. Everyone set-up Git, got into the engine and we started tweaking around with the VR-Template from Unreal 5. I threw together a simple top-down 2D Level Layout for the artists to get some scale. Also continued to churn out modules, decided on an inital control-scheme & finished up the presentation for Sprint #1.

Day 6, 27-03-2023:
Finished the presentation for Sprint #1. It went pretty good! Afterwards we sat down with the lead programmer & artist and cobbled together a roadmap for the rest of the project. It was documented into a Word document and I am currently in the process of making it into a pretty presentation so that the team can get better overview of the planning and timeline. On Wednesday we are going to sit down with the Sound Engineers to build their piece of the roadmap as well as set the workflow for sound effects; voicelines & music production.

Day 7, 28-03-2023:
Made a Roadmap for the project. This is going to be a living roadmap which will adapt slightly and it will be updated with all the sound tomorrow when we meet the sound students. The rest of the day was spent testing out scale with our props. We had some issues trying to get it right for me (short) and Anton (tall). So we found a middle ground before we found out that there are actually settings in the hardware itself to adjust floor height. Me and & George also sat down and determined the importance for some of the modules so we know what we need for the Beta Vs. Gold should be good to go!

Day 8, 29-03-2023:
Updated the roadmap to finally include Sound Engineers! We had to be rather lenient with their deadlines as they also have other school work going on at the same time. It sucks of course but it is what it is. We will try to make-do with the studio at the premise and some elbow grease to get all of the different effects and voices ready in time. Also sat down and really got some concrete sounds, voice lines & music sent into the pipeline so they can start being produced! We finally got a studio-name as well: The studio is now known as Trolley Problem Productions a logo competition is in the works and I have submitted my own attempt which I am pretty happy about. I also begun blocking out the main UI elements so that we can get some artistic and programming input on it. I am shuddering over the lack of artistic skill but it hopefully gets the point across.

Day 9, 30-03-2023:
Today I was rather stumped on what to do; but then it dawned on me: We have a sound studio in the basement. Spent most of the day doing spoof recordings, learning how to use FL Studio and producing some simple voice lines. Together with some team members (and outsourcing) I cooked up 9 sets of voicelines with some takes in between. I had some issues with the software such as the audio being recorded in mono- some recordings not going longer than 2 seconds and just general tweaking of the audio in FL being unfamilliar to me. I will continue to record lines throughout the project as we are in need of so many.

Day 10, 31-03-2023:
Felt ill so I stayed home. Prepared a Sprint Presentation and had worked on some personal prototypes. Not very productive on Alien Intern for my part.

Day 11, 03-04-2023:
In the morning I finalised the Sprint Presentation for today with animations and showcasing some of the work we have been doing during the week. It went really well and I am incredibly happy with the team's efforts through the last week we got some feedback about the layout, specifically regarding the roadmap and layout of the sprint. Instead of showing our deadlines in the roadmap we are supposed to show instead what we have been working on and what we're working on..so we'll be making a different kind of roadmap to show, not deadlines, but ..current lines?

Day 12, 04-04-2023:
Made an alpha presentation for tomorrow's escapades. It presents what the game is about & what we have for the showcase! Also planned out how we are going to present the Alpha in a fun and interactive way. We currently have everything but object grabbing implemented within the game which is quite reassuring. We might not have it by tomorrow but then so be it. I am in a weird mental state today so I feel like I haven't really done much, hope it passes until tomorrow.

Day 13, 05-04-2023:
After a couple of tedious hours with buggy physics, teleporting not working, UI elements being added last minute, new art being added last minute we got a working build very last second which was an epic win for the team! Me & George presented the Alpha gameplay with the Home Environment to show off the synthesizer & physics objects. Then we showed off the workplace with a couple of modules. Finally we had a little Alpha Museum in which we had assets and concept art which was not visible inside the other two rooms just to show off what everyone has been doing. It was quite a successful presentation and I am rather happy about it. Now however, I am completely drained socially and need a break. Can't wait for easter.

Day 14, 11-04-2023:
Back from birthday and easter. Back on track. Today we have sat down and made lists of priorities for animations and props. We took care of our little shadow today; he playtested the game and started to look a bit behind-the-scenes on how game development may look like. Afterwhich I sat in engine and made Module LEGO with some meshes that the artists had been cooking up. We are aiming to have a "Beta-lite" on Friday with a full playable day and most systems somewhat in place!

Day 15, 12-04-2023:
Today has been an exhausting day. I started working on the UI implementation but as I had gotten into the workflow I was interrupted as there had been a misunderstanding in how we were dealing with UI, so I scrapped what I had done and worked together with our responsible UI programmer and made sure that they had all the information and layouts ready so that they could make the functionality purely in C++ to further optimize our game. After that adaptive maneuvre I also had to deal with a dispute between our level Designer and another designer where they could not agree on which layout would be best for the game. I had them try it out in-engine instead of verbally trying to explain which went nowhere. George also taught me about physics constraints and a good workflow for me to be able to further aid in implementation of the modules.

Day 16, 13-04-2023:
Today I have been working mostly with modules, trying to get it to work but with the current way we have our buttons I have been struggling a little bit. But slowly I learned how to make it work nicely! I was working on a gameloop flowchart.

Day 17, 24-04-2023:
Back at the game project after losing a week to Companies having talks last week. That in itself was very insightful but now we need to replan and reassess the backlog. The morning was spent creating a physical Post-It Backlog. It looks very nice and it is going to be satisfying to move the notes onec the status changes on whatever is being worked on. I would lie if I didn't say that I had been feelign very stressed and anxious about the whole thing but it hopefully gets better throughout the week. I spent a lot of time today preparing for the sprint, and also to set-up our sound studio which now is located just next to our normal station, as in across the room. It is pretty terrible but we'll make-do. I also started to further implement the UI visuals to make it pop.

Day 18, 25-04-2023:
I recorded a multitude of voicelines today, everything from the Alien Assistant to pesky neighbours & an awesome political campaign. This is going to be really interesting to work with and edit. Having the sound studio in the same room as we work in creates a funny little issue with background noise but there's nothing we can do barring noisegates. I have cleaned up the audio to the best of my ability. As a funny little sidequest I asked everyone in our group and the other groups in the building to come up with random voicelines so we can have a larger variety in sounds and content! Day was cut short by mandatory(tm) Open-House visitation at Tenstar.

Day 19, 26-04-2023:
Recorded the final voicelines for the BETA today. Two variations of the important boss call & cut and edited the voicelines from yesterday. I also sorted out all the UI menus so that they have a good layout in-engine & Markus can start making my shitty designer art into a pretty masterpiece. I feel a lot of less stress today compared to Monday where I nearly fainted.

Day 20, 27-04-2023:
Such a clusterF' of implementing sounds with bouncing around checked out files in Unreal, an audio system that doesn't really work and different opinions have culminated today to become one of the most productive days yet. Tomorrow is BETA and we feel rather ready. It is just a BETA presentation remaining. I am anxious as hell but, it just has to work. Final changes tomorrow and the deadline is 13.30. Please work.

Day 21, 28-04-2023:
BETA! Another stressful presentation day with a million things needing to be implemented & planning gone awry by silly bugs. Despite having a backup build we decided to stress it and make an updated build same day. We added updated menus, modules, gameflow and added som QOL changes to really make the game pop for the presentation. The presentation itself ended up being a mixed bag. I was nervous in front of our esteemed guests and forgot some important information regarding the game but we managed to salvage it with some gameplay even though that was also lacking in some departments. I am going on break now for Valborg. I am going to need it.

Day 22, 02-05-2023:
Back from a long refreshing weekend. Today has mostly been spent adding everything that is needed for the finished game onto our tasklist and getting the tasks ready for delegation. We had a visit from Stunlock Studios which interrupted our workflow but it was so insightful that I was not really bothered by it. I have been designing a credits menu to be able to clearly give credit where credit is due to our lovely developers.

Day 23, 03-05-2023:
I have been working on reformatting and updating the GDD so it is completely up to date. I also acted as major game tester speed testing multiple modules and their variables to make sure that they feel good!

Day 26, 08-05-2023:
Prepared Sprint today. We were shunned a lot for a lack of feedback in our game so me and Hari have decided to do some planned crunch Wednesday & Thursday to improve the feedback significantly. Today has been spent preparing a sprint presentation and trying to fix a weird issue with physics constraints where they sometimes unparent the component they try to attach. Trying to find fix!

Day 27, 09-05-2023:
Today I altered the Wrist Menu and made most of the sounds into cues. It went fairly well. I also started on a system to change the light in the VR environment when the stockmarket is crashing. I also stayed late to re-record all the voice lines of the Virtual Assistant as they were quite outdated due to iterations of modules.

Day 28, 10-05-2023:
Managed to fix an issue with the Distance-Grab-Tractorbeam-VFX where it would not kill itself after not being called. Also worked on updating the virtual scrum board as it was in quite the state of disrepair. Did some more voicelines that I did not have time for yesterday. We had an issue where our modules would start as their tutorial voicelines were playing. But as we are using a form of timeline I suggested that we could pause it and use the delegate of the audio finish unpause the timeline and that was apparently a revolutionary thought. I felt pretty proud coming up with that solution.

Day 29, 11-05-2023:
Added the new reworked lines to engine. Today I was fiddling with haptic feedback curves, I don't fully understand how they work but the amplitude & frequency settings should become apparent as I play around with them more and more. I also rotated some of our fake lights to make the shadowcapture work properly. I also added some textures for the Gymbro table to accomodate for it using a mouth to speak now!

Day 30, 12-05-2023:
Today I started what I refer to as the Great Sound Renaissance, where I take inventory on all our sound effects & voicelines and make sure that they have the correct settings. The settings that need to be applied are Sound Classes, Attenuation Settings, Envelope Voice Chains & Virtulization Mode. This is a pretty large undertaking but should not take me too long thanks to unreal's batch editing of soundwaves. I also pushed up some voicelines that I had forgotten to..whoops!

Day 31, 15-05-2023:
The Great Sound Renaissance has been concluded. All sounds were updated with their appropriate settings. We noticed an issue with our mouths changing texture as they're talking. We changed it so that the textures no longer uses texture streaming and that fixed the issue; the characters no longer go blurry when changing texture. More bugs have been fixed on my part, I made it so that the alarm resets when you reset the day, and I also added another icon above the modules to indicate that they are about to start, and one that indicates that they are started. This should be enough feedback for the players, with two sets of icons, a voiceline and colour feedback on interactables. I hope. We also threw in confetti & a text for when you win the day or if you would lose the day!

Day 32, 16-05-2023:
I updated the modules with module arrows and clocks having them activate proper and having them located at a nice location in relation to their respective modules! I also set up so that we have a new song in the main menu and the whole team rejoiced after listening to the same theme for 8 weeks! It was quite uplifting actually.

Day 33, 17-05-2023:
The final day before GOLD. I spent the day triple checking all the sounds and playtesting a whole lot. I also helped Hari record for the trailer. This is going good, we can make this.

Day 34, 19-05-2023:
| GOLD PRESENTATION |
Today we held our presentations for the representatives at SONY, the school and anyone else who had the pleasure of being around. It went really well for all the groups, so much hard work has gone into these projects its inspiring. Some of the feedback we got from the presentation was that we should lean more into the bite-sized gameplay our game offers as it is good for the VR format. We held some playtests just after and I am so proud over the group's effort and the product we made during the 9 weeks.

Day 18, 09-10-2023:

Day 19, 10-10-2023:
Spent the day adding more feedback to the UI and changing some things for poor Albin. Now the glide has a max-timer and UI to boot which makes it pretty nice.

Day 26, 17-10-2023:
I am not gonna update every day anymore, got sick and tired of it. Sorry not sorry Fanny :)

Since last update I have been working on a drop shadow for the avatar as to make it easier for the players to determine where they will land. Started working on a hook-point, which the player can use to traverse using the fishing rod, I have a feeling it will be scrapped as it really doesn't do much that the current player movement already don't do.

We replaced the dreamcatchers with what internally is called "gay container", its a vial of rainbow liquid which contains childrens' hopes and dreams.

There has also been work at implementing "KawaiiPhysics" to make the hair of the avatar swing and react to movement without animators needing to spend a lot of time doing so.

02-3-11-2023:
Post-Beta Update.

Since last time I have been hard at working making the start & options menu of the game. They are still having slight issues but they are mostly working as intended. Some issues I have encountered have been with timing and restricting animations to their expected timings and to not have them clip with each other. One way we optimized these animations was by making a custom animation macro instead of utilizing timelines which are very expensive.

I have also been implementing the player abilities properly by stripping them off the base-player and then being able to give them with the help of pick-ups. This also includes fishin in ponds to get them which I also have been implementing the animations and logic for. It is missing the actual fish spawning as we wanted it to have a nice little floaty animation which I need to steal some math for.

I have also been hard at work getting my pickups to work in multiplayer and matching the UI animations to both players. And other general small bugfixes and implementation of feedback such as "Hold Shift to Walk", less delay on VFX spawning & making some collisions simpler to improve performance.
